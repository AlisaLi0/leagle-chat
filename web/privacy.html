Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information JuriCodex (“we,” at juricodex.online) collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using the Service, you agree to this Policy.

1. Information we collect

  • Account info (via sign-in provider). When you sign in with a provider such as Google or X (formerly Twitter), we receive your basic profile: your email address, name, and avatar image. We use this to create and identify your account. We never receive or store your provider password. If a provider does not release a verified email address to us (X may not), we generate an internal placeholder identifier for your account; you can add a real email later so that purchases and account recovery work reliably.
  • Your research. The legal questions you ask and the research sessions you save are stored so you can return to them across devices.
  • Usage and billing. We keep a count of how many research questions you use per month (to enforce plan limits). Payments are handled by our payments provider, Freemius; we do not receive or store your full card details.
  • Technical data. Standard request data (such as IP address and timestamps) used for security and rate limiting.

2. How we use information

To provide and operate the Service: authenticate you, save and show your research, enforce plan limits, process purchases through Freemius, secure the Service against abuse, and communicate with you about your account. We do not sell your personal information.

3. Google user data

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We only request your email and basic profile, use them solely to provide JuriCodex’s account and research features, and do not transfer or use this data for advertising.

4. X (Twitter) user data

When you sign in with X, we request only your basic profile (user id, name, username, avatar) and, where you permit it, your email address, via X’s OAuth 2.0 authorization. We use this solely to create and identify your account. We do not read your timeline, post on your behalf, access your direct messages, or use X data for advertising. You can revoke JuriCodex’s access at any time from your X account’s “Connected apps” settings.

5. Cookies

JuriCodex uses a single essential, first-party cookie to keep you signed in (a signed, HTTP-only session cookie), plus a short-lived cookie during the sign-in round-trip to protect against cross-site request forgery. We do not use advertising, analytics, or third-party tracking cookies. Because the session cookie is strictly necessary to operate the Service, signing in requires it; you can clear cookies or sign out at any time, and your browser settings let you block cookies (which will prevent sign-in).

6. Sharing

We share data only with service providers that help us run the Service — our sign-in providers (Google, X), payments provider (Freemius), and hosting infrastructure — and only as needed to operate it, or when required by law. We do not sell or rent your personal information.

7. Retention & deletion

We keep account and research data for as long as your account is active. You can delete saved research sessions at any time from within the Service. You may also request deletion of your entire account and all associated personal data by contacting us through the Service; we will action verified deletion requests within 30 days, except where we are required to retain certain records (for example, transaction records our payments provider must keep for tax or anti-fraud purposes). Deleting your account removes your saved research and profile data from our active systems.

8. Your privacy rights (GDPR / CCPA)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete the personal data we hold about you, to object to or restrict certain processing, and to withdraw consent. If you are in the European Economic Area or the UK, our legal bases for processing are performing our contract with you (providing the Service), our legitimate interests (security and preventing abuse), and your consent where applicable. If you are a California resident, we do not sell or share your personal information as those terms are defined by the CCPA/CPRA, and we will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights. To exercise any right, contact us through the Service; we may need to verify your identity first, and you may appeal a decision or complain to your local data-protection authority.

9. Security

We use HTTPS, signed session cookies, and standard safeguards to protect your data. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information.

10. Children

JuriCodex is not directed to children under 13 (or the age required by your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect their data.

11. Changes

We may update this Policy. Continued use after changes take effect means you accept the revised Policy.

12. Contact

Questions about this Policy, or to exercise a privacy right, can be sent through the Service.
