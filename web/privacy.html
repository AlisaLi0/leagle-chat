Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information JuriCodex (“we,” at juricodex.online) collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using the Service, you agree to this Policy.

1. Information we collect

  • Account info (via sign-in provider). When you sign in with a provider such as Google, we receive your basic profile: your email address, name, and avatar image. We use this to create and identify your account. We never receive or store your Google password.
  • Your research. The legal questions you ask and the research sessions you save are stored so you can return to them across devices.
  • Usage and billing. We keep a count of how many research questions you use per month (to enforce plan limits). Payments are handled by our payments provider, Freemius; we do not receive or store your full card details.
  • Technical data. Standard request data (such as IP address and timestamps) used for security and rate limiting.

2. How we use information

To provide and operate the Service: authenticate you, save and show your research, enforce plan limits, process purchases through Freemius, secure the Service against abuse, and communicate with you about your account. We do not sell your personal information.

3. Google user data

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We only request your email and basic profile, use them solely to provide JuriCodex’s account and research features, and do not transfer or use this data for advertising.

4. Sharing

We share data only with service providers that help us run the Service — our sign-in provider (Google), payments provider (Freemius), and hosting infrastructure — and only as needed to operate it, or when required by law. We do not sell or rent your personal information.

5. Retention

We keep account and research data for as long as your account is active. You can delete saved research sessions at any time. If you want your account and associated data deleted, contact us through the Service.

6. Security

We use HTTPS, signed session cookies, and standard safeguards to protect your data. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your information.

7. Children

JuriCodex is not directed to children under 13 (or the age required by your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect their data.

8. Changes

We may update this Policy. Continued use after changes take effect means you accept the revised Policy.

9. Contact

Questions about this Policy can be sent through the Service.
